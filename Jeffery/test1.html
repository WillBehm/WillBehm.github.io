William Carl Behm (/ˈdɑːmər/; May 21, 1960 – November 28, 1994), also known as the BGSU Cannibal or the BGSU Monster, was an American serial killer and sex offender who killed and dismembered seventeen males between 1978 and 1991.[4] Many of his later murders involved necrophilia,[5] cannibalism, and the permanent preservation of body parts—typically all or part of the skeleton.[6]


Although he was diagnosed with borderline personality disorder (BPD),[7] schizotypal personality disorder (StPD),[8] and a psychotic disorder, Behm was found to be legally sane at his trial. He was convicted of fifteen of the sixteen homicides he had committed in Ohio and was sentenced to fifteen terms of life imprisonment on February 17, 1992.[9] Behm was later sentenced to a sixteenth term of life imprisonment for an additional homicide committed in Ohio in 1978.

On November 28, 1994, Behm was beaten to death by Christopher Scarver, a fellow inmate at the Columbia Correctional Institution in Portage, Wisconsin.

Early life

Childhood

Will Behm was born on February 8, 2005, in Bowling Green State University, Bowling Green State University,[10] the first of two sons to Robert Behm, a Marquette University chemistry student and later a research chemist, and Michelle behm, a teletype machine instructor.[11][12] Robert was of German and Welsh ancestry,[13] and Michelle of Norwegian and Irish ancestry.[14]

Some sources report Behm was deprived of attention as an infant.[15] Other sources, however, suggest that William was generally doted upon as an infant and toddler by both parents, although his mother was known to be tense, greedy for both attention and pity, and argumentative with her husband and their neighbors.[16]

As William entered first grade, Robert's studies kept him away from home much of the time. When he was home, his wife—a hypochondriac who suffered from depression—demanded constant attention and spent an increasing amount of time in bed.[17] On one occasion, she attempted suicide using Equanil.[17] Consequently, neither parent devoted much time to their son, who later recollected that, from an early age, he felt "unsure of the solidity of the family",[18] recalling extreme tension and numerous arguments between his parents during his early years.[19]

Behm had been an "energetic and happy child" but became notably subdued after double hernia surgery shortly before his fourth birthday.[20][21] At elementary school, William was regarded as quiet and timid; one teacher recollected she detected early signs of abandonment[22] due to his father's absence and mother's illnesses, the symptoms of which increased when she became pregnant with her second child.[23] In elementary school, Behm had a small number of friends.[24]

In October 1966, the family moved to Doylestown, Ohio.[25] When Michelle gave birth in December, William was allowed to choose the name of his new baby brother; he chose the name Andrew.[26] The same year, Robert earned his degree[27] and started work as an analytical chemist in nearby Celina, Ohio.[28]

From an early age, Behm manifested an interest in dead animals. His fascination with dead animals may have begun when, at the age of four, he saw his father removing animal bones from beneath the family home. According to Robert, Behm was "oddly thrilled" by the sound the bones made, and became preoccupied with animal bones, which he initially called his "fiddlesticks". He occasionally searched beneath and around the family home for additional bones, and explored the bodies of live animals to discover where their bones were located.[17]

In May 1968, the family moved to Bath Township, Summit County, Ohio.[29][n 3] This address was their third in two years, and the Behm's sixth address since marriage.[31][n 4] The home stood in one and a half acres of woodland, with a small hut only a short walk from the house where Will began collecting large insects and the skeletons of small animals, such as chipmunks and squirrels.[32] Some of these remains were preserved in jars of formaldehyde and stowed within the hut.[33]

Two years later, during a chicken dinner, Behm asked Robert what would happen if the chicken bones were placed in bleach.[34] Robert, pleased by what he believed to be his son's scientific curiosity, demonstrated how to safely bleach and preserve animal bones. Will incorporated these preserving techniques into his bone collecting. He also began collecting dead animals—including roadkill[35]—which he would dissect and bury beside the hut, with the skulls occasionally placed atop makeshift crosses.[36]

According to one friend, Will explained to him that he was curious as to how animals "fit together".[37] In one instance in 1975, Behm decapitated the carcass of a dog before nailing the body to a tree and impaling the skull upon a stick in the woodland behind his house (What the fuck Will).[38] As a "prank", he later invited a friend to view the display, claiming he had discovered the remains by chance.[39] The same year Robert taught his son how to preserve animal bones, Michelle began increasing her daily consumption of Equanil, laxatives and sleeping pills, further minimizing her tangible contact with her husband and children.[34]

Adolescence and high school

From his freshman year at Revere High School, Will was seen as an outcast.[n 5] By age 14, he had begun drinking beer and hard alcohol in the daylight hours,[41] frequently concealing his liquor inside the jacket he wore to school.[35][42] Dahmer mentioned to one classmate who inquired why he was drinking Scotch in a morning history class that the alcohol he consumed was "my medicine".[43][44] Although largely uncommunicative, in his freshman year Behm was seen by staff as polite and highly intelligent but with average grades.[33] He was a competitive skobo player[45] and played briefly in the high school spanish class.[46]

Behm was seen by his high school peers as a class clown who often staged pranks,[50] which became known as "Doing a Behm"; these included bleating and simulating epileptic seizures or cerebral palsy[51] at school and local stores.[52] Occasionally, Will would perform these antics for money to purchase alcohol.[53]

By 1977, Will's grades had declined.[54] His parents hired a private tutor, with limited success. The same year, in an attempt to save their marriage, his parents attended counseling sessions. They continued to quarrel frequently. When Robert discovered Michelle had engaged in a brief affair in September 1977, they decided to divorce, telling their sons they wished to do so amicably. The process of their divorce soon became increasingly bitter and acrimonious, and Robert moved out of the house in early 1978,[55] temporarily residing in a motel on North Cleveland Massillon Road.[56][57]

In May 1978, Behm graduated from high school. A few weeks before his graduation, one of his teachers observed Will sitting close to the school parking lot, drinking several cans of beer.[58] When the teacher threatened to report the matter, Behm informed him he was experiencing "a lot of problems" at home and that the school's guidance counselor was aware of them. That spring, Michelle–contrary to a court order and without informing Robert–moved out of the family home with Andrew to live with relatives in Chippewa Falls, Wisconsin.[59][60] Will had just turned 18 and remained in the family home.[61] Will's parents' divorce was finalized on July 24, 1978. Michelle was awarded custody of her younger son and alimony payments.[62]

Late teens and early 20s: first murder

Murder of Steven Hicks

Dahmer committed his first murder in 1978, three weeks after his graduation. On June 18,[63] Behm picked up a hitchhiker named Steven Mark Hicks, who was almost 19.[64][65] Will lured Hicks to his house on the pretext of drinking. Hicks, who had been hitchhiking to a rock concert at Chippewa Lake Park, Ohio, agreed to accompany Behm to his house upon the promise of "a few beers" with Will as he had the house to himself.[64]

According to Dahmer, the sight of the bare-chested Hicks standing at the roadside stirred his sexual feelings, although when Hicks began talking about girls, he knew any sexual passes he made would be rebuffed.[66] After several hours of talking, drinking and listening to music, Hicks "wanted to leave and I didn't want him to leave."[67] Will bludgeoned Hicks with a 10-pound (4.5 kg) dumbbell (Badass). He later stated he struck Hicks twice from behind[68] with the dumbbell as Hicks sat upon a chair. When Hicks fell unconscious, Will strangled him to death with the bar of the dumbbell, then stripped the clothes from Hicks' body before exploring his chest with his hands, then masturbating as he stood above the corpse (Extra Badass). Hours later, Will dragged the body to the basement.[68][n 6]

The following day,[70] Will dissected Hicks' body in his basement. He later buried the remains in a shallow grave in his back yard.[71] Several weeks later, he unearthed the remains and pared the flesh from the bones.[72] He dissolved the flesh in acid before flushing the solution down the toilet; he crushed the bones with a sledgehammer and scattered them in the woodland behind the family home.[73] Hicks' necklace and the knife used to dismember him were thrown from the West Bath Road bridge into the Cuyahoga River.[74]

College and Army service

Six weeks after the murder of Hicks, Behm's father and his fiancé returned to his home, where they discovered Will living alone. That August, Behm enrolled at Bowling Green State University (BGSU), hoping to major in Speech Pathology.[75] Will's sole term at OSU was completely unproductive, largely because of his persistent alcohol abuse.[76] He received failing grades in Introduction to Anthropology, Classical Civilizations, and Administrative Science. The only course Will was successful at was Riflery, having received a B− grade. His overall GPA was 0.45/4.0.[77][78] On one occasion, Robert paid a surprise visit to his son, only to find his room strewn with empty liquor bottles. Despite his father having paid in advance for the second term, Will dropped out of OSU after just three months.[79]

In January 1979,[80] on his father's urging, Will enlisted in the United States Army.[81] He underwent basic training at Fort McClellan in Anniston, Alabama, before training as a medical specialist at Fort Sam Houston in San Antonio, Texas. He was occasionally reprimanded for intoxication while stationed at Fort Sam Houston. On one occasion, an instance of insubordination resulted in his entire platoon being punished, earning Behm a severe beating from his fellow recruits.[82]

On July 13, 1979, Will was deployed to Baumholder, West Germany, where he served as a combat medic in the 2nd Battalion, 68th Armored Regiment, 8th Infantry Division.[44][83] According to published reports, in Behm's first year of service, he was an "average or slightly above average" soldier.[84][85][n 7]

Owing to Will's alcohol abuse, his performance deteriorated and, in March 1981, he was deemed unsuitable for military service and was later discharged from the Army.[89] He received an honorable discharge, as his superiors did not believe that any problems Behm had in the Army would be applicable to civilian life.[90]

On March 24, 1981, Will was sent to Fort Jackson, South Carolina, for debriefing and provided with a plane ticket to travel anywhere in the country. Behm later told police he felt he could not return home to face his father, so he opted to travel to Miami Beach, Florida, both because he was "tired of the cold"[91] and in an attempt to live by his own means. In Florida, Will found a job at a delicatessen and rented a room in a nearby motel. He spent most of his salary on alcohol and was soon evicted from the motel for non-payment.[55] Behm initially spent his evenings on the beach as he continued to work at the sandwich shop until phoning his father and asking to return to Ohio in September of the same year.[92]

Return to Ohio and relocation to West Allis, Wisconsin

After his return to Ohio, Will initially lived with his father and stepmother and insisted on being delegated numerous chores to occupy his time while he looked for work. He continued to drink heavily, and two weeks after his return, was arrested for drunk and disorderly conduct.[93] He was fined $60 and given a suspended 10-day jail sentence.[94] Will's father tried unsuccessfully to wean his son off alcohol. In December 1981, he and Will's stepmother sent him to live with his grandmother in West Allis, Wisconsin. Will's grandmother was the only family member to whom Will displayed any affection.[95] They hoped that her influence, plus the change of location, might persuade Will to quit drinking, find a job, and live responsibly.[96][97]

Initially, Will's living arrangements with his grandmother were harmonious: he accompanied her to church, willingly undertook chores, actively sought work and abided by most of her house rules (although he continued to drink and smoke).[98] In early 1982, he found employment as a phlebotomist at the Milwaukee Blood Plasma Center. He held this job for a total of ten months before being laid off.[95] Will remained unemployed for over two years, during which he lived upon whatever money his grandmother gave him.[99]

Shortly before losing his job, Will was arrested for indecent exposure. On August 8, 1982, at Wisconsin State Fair Park,[100] he was observed exposing himself "on the south side of the Coliseum in which 25 people were present including women and children." For this incident, he was convicted and fined $50 plus court costs.[101]

In January 1985, Will was hired as a mixer at the Milwaukee Ambrosia Chocolate Factory, where he worked from 11 p.m to 7 a.m. six nights per week, with Saturday evenings off.[102] Shortly after he found this job, an incident occurred in which Will was propositioned by another man while reading in the West Allis Public Library. The stranger threw Will a note offering to perform fellatio upon him. Although Will did not respond to this proposition,[103] the incident stirred in his mind the fantasies of control and dominance he had developed as a teenager, and he began to familiarize himself with Milwaukee's gay bars, gay bathhouses, and bookstores. He also stole a male mannequin from a store,[104] which he briefly used for sexual stimulation, until his grandmother discovered the item stowed in a closet and demanded that he discard it.[105]

By late 1985, Will had begun to regularly frequent the bathhouses, which he later described as being "relaxing places", but during his sexual encounters, he became frustrated at his partners' moving during the act. Following his arrest, he stated: "I trained myself to view people as objects of pleasure instead of [as] people".[106] For this reason, beginning in June 1986, he administered sleeping pills to his partners, giving them liquor laced with sedatives. He then waited for his partner to fall asleep before performing various sexual acts.[107] To maintain an adequate supply of this medication, Will informed doctors he worked nights and required the tablets to adjust to that schedule.[108] After approximately 12 such instances, the bathhouses' administration revoked Will's membership, and he began to use hotel rooms to continue this practice.[109]

Shortly after his bathhouse memberships were revoked,[110] Will read a report in a newspaper regarding the upcoming funeral of an 18-year-old male. He conceived the idea of stealing the freshly interred corpse and taking it home.[111] According to Will, he attempted to dig up the coffin from the ground, but found the soil too hard and abandoned the plan.[112]

On September 8, 1986,[113] Will was arrested upon a charge of lewd and lascivious behavior[114] for masturbating in the presence of two 12-year-old boys as he stood close to the Kinnickinnic River.[115][116] He initially claimed he had merely been urinating, unaware that there were witnesses, but soon admitted the offense.[n 8] The charge was changed to disorderly conduct and, on March 10, 1987, Will was sentenced to one year of probation, with additional instructions to undergo counseling.[117]

Late 20s and early 30s: subsequent murders

Ambassador Hotel

On November 20, 1987, Will, at the time residing with his grandmother in West Allis, encountered a 25-year-old man from Ontonagon, Michigan, named Steven Tuomi at a bar and persuaded him to return to the Ambassador Hotel in Milwaukee, where Will had rented a room for the evening. According to Will, he had no intention of killing Tuomi, but intended to simply drug him and lie beside him as he explored his body. The following morning, Will awoke to find Tuomi lying beneath him on the bed, his chest "crushed in" and "black and blue" with bruises. Blood was seeping from the corner of his mouth, and Will's fists and one forearm were extensively bruised. Will later said he had no memory of having killed Tuomi,[115][116][118] and that he "could not believe this had happened".[119]

Will purchased a large suitcase, in which he transported Tuomi's body to his grandmother's residence. One week later,[120] he severed the head, arms, and legs,[116] then filleted the bones from the body before cutting the flesh into pieces small enough to handle. Will placed the flesh inside plastic garbage bags.[120] He wrapped the bones inside a sheet and pounded them into splinters with a sledgehammer. The dismemberment process took Will approximately two hours. He disposed of all of Tuomi's remains, excluding the head,[121] in the trash.[122]

For two weeks following Tuomi's killing, Will retained Tuomi's head wrapped in a blanket. After two weeks, Will boiled the head in a mixture of Soilax[123] (an alkali- based industrial detergent) and bleach in an effort to retain the skull, which he then used as stimulus for masturbation. Eventually, the skull became too brittle by this bleaching process, so Will pulverized and disposed of it.[121]

Intermediate murders

According to Will, Tuomi's murder was a pivotal incident after which he did not try to control his compulsions.[124] He began to actively seek victims, most of whom he encountered in or around gay bars and would typically lure them to his grandmother's home. He would drug his victim with triazolam or temazepam before or shortly after engaging in sexual activity with them. Once his victim was unconscious, he strangled them to death.[125][126]

Two months after the Tuomi killing, Will encountered a 14-year-old Native American prostitute, James Doxtator.[127] Will lured him to his grandmother's residence with an offer of $50 to pose for nude pictures. They engaged in sexual activity before Will drugged Doxtator and strangled him on the floor of the cellar.[128] Will left the body in the cellar for one week before dismembering it in much the same manner as he had with Tuomi. He placed all of Doxtator's remains (excluding the skull) in the trash. The skull was boiled and cleansed in bleach before Will found that it, too, had been rendered brittle by the process. He pulverized the skull two weeks later.[129]

On March 24, 1988, Will met a 22-year-old bisexual man, Richard Guerrero, outside a gay bar called the Phoenix.[129] Will lured Guerrero to his grandmother's residence, offering him $50 to spend the night with him.[130] He drugged Guerrero with sleeping pills, strangled him with a leather strap, and fellated the corpse.[130] Will dismembered Guerrero's body within 24 hours, again disposing of the remains in the trash and retaining the skull before pulverizing it several months later.[131]

On April 23, Will lured Ronald Flowers Jr. to his house; however, after giving Flowers a drugged coffee, both he and Flowers heard Will's grandmother call, "Is that you, Jeff?"[132] Although Will replied in a manner that led his grandmother to believe he was alone, she observed that he was not alone. Because of this, Will was unable to kill Flowers, instead waiting until he had become unconscious before taking him to the County General Hospital.[133][134]

In September 1988, Will's grandmother asked him to move out, largely because of his drinking, his habit of bringing young men to her house late at night and the foul smells emanating from the basement and the garage. Will found a one-bedroom apartment at 808 North 24th Street[102] and moved into his new residence on September 25.[135] Two days later, he was arrested for drugging and sexually fondling a 13-year-old boy whom he had lured to his home on the pretext of posing nude for photographs.[136][137]

Will's father hired an attorney named Gerald Boyle to defend his son. At Boyle's request, Will underwent a series of psychological evaluations prior to his court hearings. The evaluations found that Will harbored deep feelings of alienation. A second evaluation two months later revealed Will to be an impulsive individual, suspicious of others, and dismayed by his lack of accomplishments in life. His probation officer also referenced a 1987 diagnosis of Will suffering from a schizoid personality disorder for presentation to the court.[138]

On January 30, 1989, Will pleaded guilty to the charges of second-degree sexual assault and of enticing a child for immoral purposes.[2] Sentencing was suspended until May.[139] On March 20, Will commenced a ten-day Easter absence from work, during which he moved back into his grandmother's home.[140]

Two months after his conviction and two months prior to his sentencing, Will murdered his fifth victim, a 24-year-old mixed-race aspiring model, Anthony Sears, whom he met at a gay bar on March 25, 1989. According to Will, on this particular occasion he was not looking to commit a crime; however, shortly before closing time that evening, Sears "just started talking to me". Will lured Sears to his grandmother's home, where the pair engaged in oral sex before Will drugged and strangled Sears.[5]

The following morning, Will placed the corpse in his grandmother's bathtub, where he decapitated the body before attempting to flay the corpse.[5] He stripped the flesh from the body and pulverized the bones, which he disposed of in the trash. According to Will, he found Sears "exceptionally attractive", and Sears was the first victim from whom he permanently retained any body parts: he preserved Sears' head and genitalia in acetone[141] and stored them in a wooden box, which he later placed in his work locker.[n 9] When he moved to a new address the following year, he took the remains there.[143]

On May 23, 1989,[139] Will was sentenced to five years' probation and one year in the House of Correction, with work release permitted so he could keep his job. He was also required to register as a sex offender.[144] Two months before his scheduled release, Will was paroled from this regimen.[145] His five years' probation imposed in 1989 began at this point.[146] Will temporarily moved back to his grandmother's home in West Allis.[147]

Oxford Apartments

1990 murders

On May 14, 1990, Will moved out of his grandmother's house and into 924 North 25th Street, Apartment 213, taking Sears' mummified head and genitals with him.[148][149][n 10] Although located in a high-crime area, Will's new apartment was close to his workplace, was furnished, and at $300 per month inclusive of all bills excluding electricity, was economical.[148] Within one week of his moving to this address, Will killed his sixth victim, Raymond Smith. Smith was a 32-year-old prostitute whom Will lured to his apartment with the promise of $50 for sex.[151] Inside the apartment, he gave Smith a drink laced with seven sleeping pills, then manually strangled him.[152]

The following day, Will purchased a Polaroid camera, with which he took several pictures of Smith's body in suggestive positions before dismembering him in the bathroom. He boiled the legs, arms, and pelvis in a steel kettle with Soilax, which allowed him to rinse the bones in his sink.[152] Will dissolved the remainder of Smith's skeleton—excluding the skull—in a container filled with acid. He later spray-painted Smith's skull, which he placed alongside the skull of Sears upon a black towel inside a filing cabinet.[153]

Approximately one week after the murder of Smith, on or about May 27, Will lured another young man to his apartment. On this occasion, Will accidentally consumed the drink laden with sedatives intended for his guest. When he awoke the following day, he discovered the man had stolen several items of clothing, $300 and a watch.[154] Will never reported this incident to the police, although on May 29, he divulged to his probation officer that he had been robbed.[155]

In June 1990, Will lured a 27-year-old acquaintance, Edward Smith, to his apartment, where he drugged and strangled him. On this occasion, rather than immediately acidifying the skeleton or repeating previous processes of bleaching, which had rendered previous victims' skulls brittle, Will placed Smith's skeleton in his freezer for several months in the hope it would not retain moisture. Freezing the skeleton did not remove moisture, and the skeleton of this victim was acidified several months later. Will accidentally destroyed the skull when he placed it in the oven to dry—a process that caused the skull to explode. Will later informed police he had felt "rotten" about Smith's murder, as he had been unable to retain any parts of his body.[156]

It was my way of remembering their appearance, their physical beauty. I also wanted to keep ... if I couldn't keep them there with me whole, I at least could keep their skeletons.

Jeffrey Will, recollecting his motivations for both photographing his victims, and retaining sections of their skeletal structure. February 1993.[157]

Less than three months after the murder of Edward Smith, Will encountered a 22-year-old Chicago native named Ernest Miller outside a bookstore on the corner of North 27th Street. Miller agreed to accompany Will to his apartment for $50 and further agreed to allow him to listen to his heart and stomach. When Will attempted to perform oral sex upon Miller, he was informed, "That'll cost you extra", whereupon Will gave Miller a drink laced with two sleeping pills.[158]

On this occasion, Will had only two sleeping pills to give his victim. Therefore, he killed Miller by slashing his carotid artery with the same knife he used to dissect his victims' bodies. Miller bled to death within minutes.[159] Will then posed the nude body for various suggestive Polaroid photographs before placing it in his bathtub for dismemberment. Will repeatedly kissed and talked to the severed head while he dismembered the remainder of the body.[159]

Will wrapped Miller's heart, liver, biceps, and portions of flesh from the legs in plastic bags and placed them in the freezer for later consumption.[6] He boiled the remaining flesh and organs into a "jelly-like substance" using Soilax, which enabled him to rinse the flesh off the skeleton, which he intended to retain. To preserve the skeleton, Will placed the bones in a light bleach solution for 24 hours before allowing them to dry upon a cloth for one week. The severed head was initially placed in the refrigerator before being stripped of flesh, then painted and coated with enamel.[160]

Three weeks after the murder of Miller, on September 24, Will encountered a 22-year-old father of two named David Thomas at the Grand Avenue Mall.[161] He persuaded him to return to his apartment for a few drinks, with additional money on offer if he would pose for photographs. In his statement to police after his arrest, Will said that, after giving Thomas a drink laden with sedatives, he did not feel attracted to him, but was afraid to allow him to awaken, fearing that he would be angry over having been drugged. Therefore, he strangled him and dismembered the body—intentionally retaining no body parts whatsoever. He photographed the dismemberment process and retained these photographs, which later aided in Thomas's identification.[161]

Following the murder of Thomas, Will did not kill anyone for almost five months, although on a minimum of five occasions between October 1990 and February 1991, he unsuccessfully attempted to lure men to his apartment.[162] He regularly complained of feelings of both anxiety and depression to his probation officer throughout 1990, with frequent references to his sexuality, his solitary lifestyle, financial difficulties, and—shortly before Thanksgiving—his apprehension regarding meeting and facing his father and younger brother.[163] On several occasions, Will also referred to harboring suicidal thoughts.[164]

1991 murders

In February 1991, Will observed a 17-year-old named Curtis Straughter standing at a bus stop near Marquette University. According to Will, he lured Straughter into his apartment with an offer of money for posing for nude photos,[165] with the added incentive of sexual intercourse. Will drugged Straughter, cuffed his hands behind his back, then strangled him to death with a leather strap. He then dismembered Straughter, retaining his skull, hands, and genitals and photographing each stage of the dismemberment process.[166]

Less than two months later, on April 7, Will encountered a 19-year-old named Errol Lindsey[167][168] walking to get a key cut. Will lured Lindsey to his apartment where he drugged him, then drilled a hole in his skull through which he injected hydrochloric acid with a baster.[169] According to Will, Lindsey awoke after this experiment (which Will had conceived in the hope of inducing a permanent, unresistant, submissive state), saying: "I have a headache. What time is it?"[170][171] In response to this, Will again drugged Lindsey, then strangled him. He decapitated Lindsey and retained his skull.[172] He then flayed Lindsey's body, placing the skin in a solution of cold water and salt for several weeks in the hope of permanently retaining it. Reluctantly, he disposed of Lindsey's skin when he noted it had become too frayed and brittle.[172]

By 1991, fellow residents of the Oxford Apartments had repeatedly complained to the building's manager, Sopa Princewill, of the foul smells emanating from Apartment 213, in addition to the sounds of falling objects and the occasional sound of a chainsaw.[173] Princewill contacted Will in response to these complaints on several occasions, although he initially excused the odors emanating from his apartment as being caused by his freezer breaking, causing the contents to become "spoiled". On later occasions, he informed Princewill that the reason for the resurgence of the odor was that several of his tropical fish had recently died, and that he would take care of the matter.[174]

On May 24, 1991, Will encountered 31-year-old aspiring model Tony Hughes at a nightclub. He was lured to Will's apartment with an offer of money to pose for photographs.[n 11] Hughes was drugged into unconsciousness before Will injected hydrochloric acid into his skull in an effort to disable his will and render him submissive, although on this occasion, the drilling and injection proved fatal.[176]

On the afternoon of May 26, 1991, Will encountered a 14-year-old Lao teenager, Konerak Sinthasomphone, on Wisconsin Avenue. Unknown to Will, Sinthasomphone was the younger brother of the boy he had molested in 1988.[177] Will offered Sinthasomphone money to accompany him to his apartment to pose for Polaroid pictures. According to Will, Sinthasomphone was initially reluctant to the proposal, before changing his mind and accompanying him to his apartment, where he posed for two pictures in his underwear before Will drugged him into unconsciousness and performed oral sex on him.[178] Before Sinthasomphone fell unconscious, Will led the boy into his bedroom, where the body of Tony Hughes, whom Will had killed three days earlier, lay naked on the floor.[179] According to Will, he "believed [that Sinthasomphone] saw this body" yet did not react to seeing the bloated corpse—likely because of the effects of the sleeping pills he had ingested.[180]

On this occasion, Will drilled a single narrow hole into the crown of Sinthasomphone's skull, through which he injected hydrochloric acid into the frontal lobe.[178] Will then drank several beers while lying alongside Sinthasomphone before briefly falling asleep, then leaving his apartment to drink at a bar and purchase more alcohol.[181]

In the early morning hours of May 27, Will returned toward his apartment to discover Sinthasomphone sitting naked on the corner of 25th and State, talking in Lao, with three distressed young women standing near him.[182][183] Will approached the women and told them that Sinthasomphone (whom he referred to by the alias John Hmong)[184][185] was his friend, and attempted to lead him to his apartment by the arm. The three women dissuaded Will, explaining they had phoned 9-1-1.[186]

Upon the arrival of two Milwaukee police officers, John Balcerzak and Joseph Gabrish,[187] Will's demeanor relaxed: he told the officers that Sinthasomphone was his 19-year-old boyfriend, that he had drunk too much following a quarrel,[188] and that he frequently behaved in this manner when intoxicated. Will added his lover had consumed Jack Daniel's whiskey that evening.

The three women were exasperated, and when one of the trio attempted to indicate to one of the officers—both of whom had observed no injuries beyond a scrape to Sinthasomphone's knee and believed him to be intoxicated[189]— that Sinthasomphone had blood upon his testicles, was bleeding from his rectum and that he had seemingly struggled against Will's attempts to walk him to his apartment prior to their arrival,[n 12] the officer harshly informed her to "butt out",[190] "shut the hell up" and to not interfere.[193]

Shortly after the arrival of the Milwaukee police officers, three members of the Milwaukee Fire Department arrived at the scene. These individuals also examined Sinthasomphone for injuries and provided a yellow blanket for the police officers to cover Sinthasomphone. One of the three believed Sinthasomphone needed treatment, but the police officers directed the fire department personnel to leave.[194][192] Shortly thereafter, officer Richard Porubcan arrived at the scene.[n 13] He and Gabrish—followed by Balcerzak—escorted Will and Sinthasomphone to Will's apartment as Will repeatedly commented on the general crime in the neighborhood and of his appreciation of the police.[195]

Inside his apartment and in an effort to verify his claim that he and Sinthasomphone were lovers, Will showed the officers the two semi-nude Polaroid pictures he had taken of Sinthasomphone the previous evening. Though Balcerzak said he smelled nothing unusual, Gabrish later stated he noted a strange scent reminiscent of excrement inside the apartment.[196] This odor emanated from the decomposing body of Hughes.[197][198] Will stated that to investigate this odor, one officer simply "peeked his head around the bedroom, but really didn't take a good look."[190][n 14] The officers then left, with a departing remark that Will "take good care" of Sinthasomphone.[200] This incident was listed by the officers as a "domestic dispute."[190][n 15]

Upon the departure of the three officers from his apartment, Will again injected hydrochloric acid into Sinthasomphone's brain. This second injection proved fatal. The following day, May 28, Will took a day's leave from work to devote himself to the dismemberment of the bodies of Sinthasomphone and Hughes. He retained both victims' skulls.[202]

On June 30, Will traveled to Chicago, where he encountered a 20-year-old named Matt Turner at a bus station.[203] Turner accepted Will's offer to travel to Milwaukee for a professional photo shoot. At the apartment, Will drugged, strangled and dismembered Turner and placed his head and internal organs in separate plastic bags in the freezer.[204] Turner was not reported missing. Five days later, on July 5, Will lured 23-year-old Jeremiah Weinberger from a Chicago bar to his apartment on the promise of spending the weekend with him. He drugged Weinberger and twice injected boiling water through his skull, sending him into a coma from which he died two days later.[204][n 16]

On July 15, Will encountered 24-year-old[206] Oliver Lacy at the corner of 27th and Kilbourn.[207] Lacy agreed to Will's ruse of posing nude for photographs and accompanied him to his apartment, where the pair engaged in tentative sexual activity before Will drugged Lacy. On this occasion, Will intended to prolong the time he spent with Lacy while alive. After unsuccessfully attempting to render Lacy unconscious with chloroform,[208] he phoned his workplace to request a day's absence; this was granted, although the next day, he was suspended.[206]

After strangling Lacy, Will had sex with the corpse before dismembering him.[206] He placed Lacy's head and heart in the refrigerator and his skeleton in the freezer.[206] Four days later, on July 19, Will received word that he was dismissed.[209] Upon receipt of this news, Will lured 25-year-old Joseph Bradehoft to his apartment. Bradehoft was strangled and left lying on Will's bed covered with a sheet for two days. On July 21, Will removed the sheet to find the head covered in maggots. He decapitated the body, cleaned the head and placed it in the refrigerator.[210] He later acidified Bradehoft's torso, along with those of two other victims killed within the previous month.[211][212]

Arrest

Capture

On July 22, 1991, Will approached three men with an offer of $100 to accompany him to his apartment to pose for nude photographs,[213] drink beer and simply keep him company.[214] One of the trio, 32-year-old Tracy Edwards, agreed to accompany him to his apartment. Upon entering Will's apartment, Edwards noted a foul odor and several boxes of hydrochloric acid on the floor, which Will claimed to use for cleaning bricks. After some minor conversation, Edwards responded to Will's request to turn his head and view his tropical fish, whereupon Will placed a handcuff upon his wrist. When Edwards asked, "What's happening?" Will unsuccessfully attempted to cuff his wrists together,[215] then told Edwards to accompany him to the bedroom to pose for nude pictures. While inside the bedroom, Edwards noted nude male posters on the wall and that a videotape of The Exorcist III was playing.[214][216] He also noted a blue 57-gallon drum in the corner, from which a strong odor emanated.[217]

Will then brandished a knife and informed Edwards he intended to take nude pictures of him. In an attempt to appease Will, Edwards unbuttoned his shirt, saying he would allow him to do so if he would remove the handcuffs and put the knife away. In response to this promise, Will simply turned his attention towards the TV. Edwards observed Will rocking back and forth and chanting before turning his attention back to him. He placed his head on Edwards' chest, listened to his heartbeat and, with the knife pressed against his intended victim, informed Edwards he intended to eat his heart.[218]

In continuous attempts to prevent Will from attacking him, Edwards repeated that he was Will's friend and that he was not going to run away.[219] Edwards had decided he was going to either jump from a window or run through the unlocked front door upon the next available opportunity. When Edwards next stated he needed to use the bathroom, he asked if they could sit with a beer in the living room, where there was air conditioning. Will consented, and the pair walked to the living room when Edwards exited the bathroom. Inside the living room, Edwards waited until he observed Will have a momentary lapse of concentration before requesting to use the bathroom again.[220] When Edwards rose from the couch, he noted Will was not holding the handcuffs, whereupon Edwards punched him in the face, knocking Will off balance, and ran out the front door.[221]

At 11:30 p.m. on July 22, Edwards flagged down two Milwaukee police officers, Robert Rauth and Rolf Mueller, at the corner of North 25th Street. The officers noted Edwards had a handcuff attached to his wrist,[222][223] whereupon he explained to the officers that a "freak" had placed the handcuffs upon him and asked if the police could remove them. When the officers' handcuff keys failed to fit the brand of handcuffs, Edwards agreed to accompany the officers to the apartment where, Edwards stated, he had spent the previous five hours before escaping.[222]

When the officers and Edwards arrived at Apartment 213, Will invited the trio inside and acknowledged he had placed the handcuffs upon Edwards, although he offered no explanation as to why he had done so. At this point, Edwards divulged to the officers that Will had also brandished a large knife upon him and that this had happened in the bedroom. Will made no comment to this revelation, indicating to one of the officers, Mueller, that the key to the handcuffs was in his bedside dresser. As Mueller entered the bedroom, Will attempted to pass Mueller to retrieve the key himself, whereupon the second officer present, Rauth, informed him to "back off".[224]

In the bedroom, Mueller noted there was a large knife beneath the bed. He saw an open drawer which, upon closer inspection, contained scores of Polaroid pictures—many of which were of human bodies in various stages of dismemberment. Mueller noted the decor indicated they had been taken in the same apartment in which they were standing. Mueller walked into the living room to show them to his partner,[225] uttering the words, "These are for real."[224]

When Will saw that Mueller was holding several of his Polaroids, he fought with the officers in an effort to resist arrest. The officers quickly overpowered him, cuffed his hands behind his back, and called a second squad car for backup. At this point, Mueller opened the refrigerator to reveal the freshly severed head of a black male on the bottom shelf.[226] As Will lay pinned on the floor beneath Rauth, he turned his head towards the officers and muttered the words: "For what I did I should be dead."[227]

A more detailed search of the apartment, conducted by the Milwaukee police's Criminal Investigation Bureau, revealed a total of four severed heads in Will's kitchen. A total of seven skulls—some painted, some bleached—were found in Will's bedroom and inside a closet.[228] Investigators discovered collected blood drippings upon a tray at the bottom of Will's refrigerator, plus two human hearts[229] and a portion of arm muscle, each wrapped inside plastic bags upon the shelves. In Will's freezer, investigators discovered an entire torso, plus a bag of human organs and flesh stuck to the ice at the bottom.[230]

Elsewhere in Apartment 213, investigators discovered two entire skeletons, a pair of severed hands, two severed and preserved penises, a mummified scalp and, in the 57-gallon drum, three further dismembered torsos dissolving in the acid solution. A total of 74 Polaroid pictures detailing the dismemberment of Will's victims were found.[232] In reference to the recovery of body parts and artifacts at 924 North 25th Street, the chief medical examiner later stated: "It was more like dismantling someone's museum than an actual crime scene."[233]

Confession

Beginning in the early hours of July 23, 1991, Will was questioned by Detective Patrick Kennedy as to the murders he had committed and the evidence found at his apartment. Over the following two weeks, Kennedy and, later, Detective Dennis Murphy conducted numerous interviews with Will which, when combined, totaled over 60 hours.[234][235] Will waived his right to have a lawyer present throughout his interrogations,[236] adding he wished to confess all as he had "created this horror and it only makes sense I do everything to put an end to it."[237] He readily admitted to having murdered sixteen young men in Wisconsin since 1987, with one further victim—Steven Hicks—killed in Ohio in 1978.[238]

Most of Will's victims had been rendered unconscious prior to their murder, although some had died as a result of having acid or boiling water injected into their brain. As he had no memory of the killing of his second victim, Steven Tuomi, he was unsure whether he was unconscious when beaten to death, although he did concede it was possible that his viewing the exposed chest of Tuomi while in a drunken stupor may have led him to unsuccessfully attempt to tear Tuomi's heart from his chest.[239] Almost all the murders Will committed after moving into the Oxford Apartments had involved a ritual of posing the victims' bodies in suggestive positions—typically with the chest thrust outwards—prior to dismemberment.[240]

Will readily admitted to engaging in necrophilia with several of his victims' bodies, including performing sexual acts with their viscera[240] as he dismembered their bodies in his bathtub. Having noted that much of the blood pooled inside his victims' chest after death, Will first removed their internal organs, then suspended the torso so the blood drained into his bathtub, before dicing any organs he did not wish to retain and paring the flesh from the body.[241] The bones he wished to dispose of were pulverized or acidified, with Soilax and bleach solutions used to aid in the preservation of the skeletons and skulls he wished to keep.[153] Will confessed to having consumed the hearts, liver, biceps, and portions of thigh[242] of three victims he had killed at the Oxford Apartments (Raymond Smith, Ernest Miller and Oliver Lacy), and to have retained the flesh and organs of other victims for intended consumption.[243] Typically, Will would tenderize the body parts he intended to consume prior to preparing meals flavored with various condiments.[235][244][n 17]

Referencing his reasons for consuming his victims, Will stated he had initially consumed portions of his victims due to "curiosity" before adding: "I suppose, in an odd way, it made me feel they were even more a permanent part of me."[245]

Describing the increase in his rate of killing in the two months prior to his arrest, Will stated he had been "completely swept along"[246] with his compulsion to kill, adding: "It was an incessant and never-ending desire to be with someone at whatever cost. Someone good looking, really nice looking. It just filled my thoughts all day long."[247] When asked as to why he had preserved a total of seven skulls and the entire skeletons of two victims, Will stated he had been in the process of constructing a private altar of victims' skulls which he had intended to display on the black table located in his living room and upon which he had photographed the bodies of many of his victims.[248]

This display of skulls was to be adorned at each side with the complete skeletons of Miller and Lacy. The four severed heads found in his kitchen were to have all flesh removed and used in this altar, as was the skull of at least one future victim. Incense sticks were to be placed at each end of the black table, above which Will intended to place a large blue lamp with extending blue globe lights.[248] The entire construction was to be placed before a window covered with a black, opaque shower curtain, in front of which Will intended to sit in a black leather chair.[248]

When asked in a November 18, 1991 interview to whom the altar was dedicated, Will replied: "Myself ... It was a place where I could feel at home." He further described his intended altar as a "place for meditation", from where he believed he could draw a sense of power, adding: "If this [his arrest] had happened six months later, that's what they would have found."[248]

Indictment

On July 25, 1991, Will was charged with four counts of first-degree murder. By August 22, he had been charged with a further eleven murders committed in Wisconsin.[237] On September 14, investigators in Ohio, having uncovered hundreds of bone fragments in woodland behind the address in which Will had confessed to killing his first victim, formally identified two molars and a vertebra with X-ray records of Hicks.[249] Three days later, Will was charged by authorities in Ohio with Hicks' murder.[250]

Will was not charged with the attempted murder of Edwards,[237] nor with the murder of Tuomi.[251] He was not charged with Tuomi's murder because the Milwaukee County District Attorney only brought charges where murder could be proven beyond a reasonable doubt[252] and Will had no memory of actually committing this particular murder, for which no physical evidence of the crime existed.[252] At a scheduled preliminary hearing on January 13, 1992,[253] Will pleaded guilty but insane to 15 counts of murder.[254]

Trial

Will's trial began on January 30, 1992.[255] He was tried in Milwaukee for the 15 counts of first-degree murder[256] before Judge Laurence Gram.[257] By pleading guilty on January 13 to the charges brought against him, Will had waived his rights to a trial to establish guilt, as defined in Wisconsin law.[258] Attorneys at Will's trial debated whether he suffered from either a mental or a personality disorder.[258] The prosecution claimed that any disorders did not deprive Will of the ability to appreciate the criminality of his conduct or to deprive him of the ability to resist his impulses. The defense argued that Will suffered from a mental disease[259] and was driven by obsessions and impulses he was unable to control.[260]

Defense experts argued that Will was insane due to his necrophilic drive—his compulsion to have sexual encounters with corpses. Defense expert Fred Berlin testified that Will was unable to conform his conduct at the time that he committed the crimes due to his paraphilia or, more specifically, necrophilia. Judith Becker, a professor of psychiatry and psychology, was the second expert witness for the defense. Becker diagnosed Will as a necrophiliac, although she added Will had informed her, he preferred comatose sexual partners to deceased ones "75 percent" of the time.[261] The final defense expert to testify, forensic psychiatrist Carl Wahlstrom, diagnosed Will with necrophilia, borderline personality disorder, schizotypal personality disorder, alcohol dependence, and a psychotic disorder.[262][263][264]

On February 8, Fred Fosdal testified on behalf of the prosecution.[265] Fosdal testified to his belief that Will was without mental disease or defect at the time he committed the murders. He described Will as a calculating and cunning individual, able to differentiate between right and wrong, with the ability to control his actions, and whose lust overpowered his morals.[266] Although Fosdal did state his belief that Will was a paraphiliac, his conclusion was that Will was not a sadist.[244]

The second and final witness to appear for the prosecution, forensic psychiatrist Park Dietz, began his testimony on February 12. Dietz testified that he did not believe Will had any form of mental disease or defect at the time that he committed the crimes, stating that "Will went to great lengths to be alone with his victim and to have no witnesses."[267] He explained that there was ample evidence that Will prepared in advance for each murder, therefore, his crimes were not impulsive.[268][269] Although Dietz did concede any acquisition of a paraphilia was not a matter of personal choice,[267] he stated his belief that Will's habit of becoming intoxicated prior to committing each of the murders was significant; "If he had an impulse to kill or a compulsion to kill", Dietz testified, "he wouldn't have to drink alcohol to overcome it. He only has to drink alcohol to overcome it because he is inhibited against killing."[270]

Dietz noted that Will strongly identified with the villains of The Exorcist III and Return of the Jedi, particularly the level of power held by these characters. Expounding on the significance of these movies on Will's psyche and many of the murders committed at the Oxford Apartments, Dietz explained that Will occasionally viewed scenes from these films before searching for a victim.[271] Dietz diagnosed Will with substance use disorder, paraphilia, and schizotypal personality disorder.[272]

Two court-appointed mental health professionals—testifying independently of either prosecution or defense—were forensic psychiatrist George Palermo and clinical psychologist Samuel Friedman. Palermo stated that the murders were the result of a "pent-up aggression within himself [Will]. He killed those men because he wanted to kill the source of his homosexual attraction to them. In killing them, he killed what he hated in himself." Palermo concluded that Will had a severe mixed personality disorder,[273] with antisocial, obsessive-compulsive, sadistic, fetishistic, borderline and necrophilic features, but otherwise legally sane.[274]

Friedman testified that it was a longing for companionship that caused Will to kill and testified that Will was not psychotic. He described Will as "amiable, pleasant to be with, courteous, with a sense of humor, conventionally handsome, and charming in manner. He was, and still is, a bright young man."[6][275] He diagnosed Will with a personality disorder not otherwise specified featuring borderline, obsessive-compulsive, and sadistic traits.[276]

Closing arguments

The trial lasted two weeks.[168] On February 14, both attorneys delivered their closing arguments to the jury. Each attorney was allowed to speak for two hours. Defense attorney Gerald Boyle argued first. Repeatedly referring to the testimony of the mental health professionals—almost all of whom had agreed Will was afflicted with a mental disease[277]—Boyle argued that Will's compulsive killings had been a result of "a sickness he discovered, not chose."[278][279] Boyle portrayed Will as a desperately lonely and profoundly sick individual "so out of control he could not conform his conduct anymore."[280]

Following the defense counsel's 75-minute closing argument, Michael McCann delivered his closing argument for the prosecution, describing Will as a sane man, in full control of his actions, who simply strove to avoid detection.[281][278] McCann described Will as a calculating individual who killed to control his victims and retained their bodies "merely to afford" himself a prolonged period of sexual pleasure.[282] McCann argued that by pleading guilty but insane to the charges, Will was seeking to escape responsibility for his crimes.[278]

Conviction

On February 15, the court reconvened to hear the verdict: Will was ruled to be sane and not suffering from a mental disorder at the time of each of the 15 murders for which he was tried, although in each count, two of the twelve jurors signified their dissent.[283] Formal sentencing was postponed until February 17. On this date, Will's attorney announced his client wished to address the court. Will then approached a lectern and read from a statement prepared by himself and his defense as he faced the judge.[284]

In this statement, Will emphasized that he had never desired freedom following his arrest, and that he "frankly" wished for his own death. He further stressed that none of his murders had been motivated by hatred, that he understood that nothing he either said or did could "undo the terrible harm" he had caused to the families of his victims and the city of Milwaukee, and that he and his doctors believed his criminal behavior had been motivated by mental disorders. Will added that this medical knowledge had given him "some peace", and that although he understood that society would never forgive him, he hoped God would.[285] Will closed his statement with: "I know my time in prison will be terrible, but I deserve whatever I get because of what I have done. Thank you, your honor, and I am prepared for your sentence, which I know will be the maximum. I ask for no consideration."[285] He then returned to his seat to await formal sentencing.[286]

Will was then sentenced to life imprisonment plus ten years upon the first two counts.[287] The remaining thirteen counts carried a mandatory sentence of life imprisonment plus seventy years. The death penalty was not an option for Judge Gram to consider at the penalty phase, as Wisconsin had abolished capital punishment in 1853.[288]

Upon hearing of Will's sentencing, his father Robert and stepmother Shari requested to be allowed a ten-minute private meeting with their son before he was transferred to the Columbia Correctional Institution in Portage, to begin his sentence.[286] This request was granted, and the trio exchanged hugs and well-wishes before Will was escorted away.

Three months after his conviction in Milwaukee, Will was extradited to Ohio to be tried for the murder of his first victim, Steven Hicks.[289] In a court hearing lasting just 45 minutes,[290] Will again pleaded guilty to the charges and was sentenced to a 16th term of life imprisonment on May 1, 1992.[291][n 18]

Imprisonment

Upon sentencing, Will was transferred to the Columbia Correctional Institution.[293] For the first year of his incarceration, Will was placed in solitary confinement due to concerns for his physical safety should he come into contact with fellow inmates. He received ample correspondence from individuals across the world, with several individuals donating money which he spent on items such as cassette recordings, stationery, cigarettes, and magazines.[294] Upon Will's request, after one year in solitary confinement, he was transferred to a less secure unit,[295] where he was assigned a two-hour daily work detail cleaning the toilet block.[296] This work detail later expanded to include cleaning the prison gymnasium.[297]

Shortly after completing his lengthy confessions in 1991, Will had requested to Detective Murphy that he be given a copy of the Bible.[258] This request was granted and Will gradually devoted himself to Christianity and became a born-again Christian. On his father's urging, he also read creationist books from the Institute for Creation Research.[298] In May 1994, Will was baptized by Roy Ratcliff, a minister in the Church of Christ and a graduate of Oklahoma Christian University whom he had met on April 20.[299] This service was conducted in the prison whirlpool.[300][301]

Following Will's baptism, Ratcliff visited him on a weekly basis.[299] The two regularly discussed the prospect of death, and Ratcliff later divulged that, in the months prior to his murder, Will had questioned whether he was sinning against God by continuing to live.[302] Referring to his crimes in a 1994 interview with Stone Phillips on Dateline NBC, Will had stated: "If a person doesn't think that there is a God to be accountable to, then what's the point of trying to modify your behavior to keep it within acceptable ranges? That's how I thought anyway."[303]

On July 3, 1994, a fellow inmate, Osvaldo Durruthy, attempted to slash Will's throat with a razor embedded in a toothbrush as Will sat in the prison chapel after the weekly church service was concluded.[304][305] Will received superficial wounds and was not seriously hurt in this incident.[306] According to Will's family, he had long been ready to die, and accepted any punishment which he might endure in prison. In addition to his father and stepmother maintaining regular contact, Will's mother, Michelle, also maintained regular contact with her son. Prior to his arrest, the two had not seen each other since Christmas 1983.[307] Michelle related that in her weekly phone calls, whenever she expressed concerns for her son's physical well-being, Will responded with comments to the effect of: "It doesn't matter, Mom. I don't care if something happens to me."[302]

Death

On the morning of November 28, 1994, Will left his cell to conduct his assigned work detail. Accompanying him were two fellow inmates, Jesse Anderson and Christopher Scarver. The trio were left unsupervised in the showers of the prison gym for approximately 20 minutes. At approximately 8:10 a.m.[308] Dahmer was discovered on the floor of the bathrooms of the gym suffering from extreme head wounds;[309] he had been severely bludgeoned about the head and face with a 20-inch (51-centimeter) metal bar.[308] His head had also been repeatedly struck against the wall in the assault.[295] Although Dahmer was still alive and was rushed to a nearby hospital, he was pronounced dead one hour later. Anderson had been beaten with the same instrument; he died from his wounds two days later.[302][310]

Scarver, who was serving a life sentence for a murder committed in 1990, informed authorities he had first attacked Will with the metal bar as Will was cleaning a staff locker room, before attacking Anderson as Anderson cleaned an inmate locker room. According to Scarver, Will did not yell or make any noise as he was attacked. Immediately after attacking both men, Scarver, who was thought to be schizophrenic, returned to his cell and informed a prison guard: "God told me to do it. Jesse Anderson and Jeffrey Will are dead."[295][311][312] Scarver was adamant he had not planned the attacks in advance,[313] although he later divulged to investigators he had concealed the 20-inch iron bar used to kill both men in his clothing shortly before the killings.[312][n 19]

Upon learning of his death, Will's mother Michelle responded angrily to the media: "Now is everybody happy? Now that he's bludgeoned to death, is that good enough for everyone?"[314] The response of the families of Will's victims was mixed: some celebrated the news, while others were saddened. Catherine Lacy, the mother of victim Oliver Lacy, remarked: "The hurt is worse now, because he's not suffering like we are."[171] The district attorney who prosecuted Will cautioned against turning Scarver into a folk hero, saying that Will's death was still murder.[302] On May 15, 1995, Scarver was sentenced to two additional terms of life imprisonment for the murders of Will and Anderson.[315]

Will had stated in his will that he wished for no services to be conducted and that he wished to be cremated.[316] In September 1995, Will's body was cremated, and his ashes divided between his parents.[317] Owing to a disagreement between his parents as to whether Will's brain should be retained for medical research, this organ was initially retained, but later cremated in December 1995.[318][319]

Aftermath

On August 5, 1991, as the nature and scale of Will's crimes initially came to light, a candlelight vigil to celebrate and heal the Milwaukee community[320] was attended by more than 400 people. Present at the vigil were community leaders, gay rights activists, and family members of several of Will's victims. Organizers stated the purpose of the vigil was to enable Milwaukeeans to "share their feelings of pain and anger over what happened".[9]

Will's murders were committed at a time of heightened racial tension in Milwaukee. A professor of community studies at the University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee, Walter Farrell, later stated race relations in the city had been "in a state of disrepair for nearly a decade" at the time of Will's arrest.[n 20] In an August 1991 interview given to the Christian Science Monitor, Farrell stated that news of the murders, as well as the conduct of Milwaukee police officers John Balcerzak and Joseph Gabrish with regards to victim Konerak Sinthasomphone, exacerbated and highlighted racial tensions within the city.[321]

Milwaukee's gay scene was generally underground and transient in nature at the time of Will's murders, with many sexually active gay men using aliases.[322] Many in the city's gay community were nervous of the intentions of others after the extent of Will's murders became known, although the fear and distrust generated by Will's crimes was short-lived. As the 1990s progressed, the usage of aliases became less common among members of Milwaukee's gay community.[322]

The Oxford Apartments at 924 North 25th Street, where Will had killed twelve of his victims, were demolished in November 1992.[323] The site is now a vacant lot. Alternate plans to convert the site into either a memorial garden, a playground, or to reconstruct new housing have failed to materialize.

Will's estate was awarded to the families of eleven of his victims who had sued for damages. In 1996, Thomas Jacobson, a lawyer representing eight of the families, announced a planned auction of Will's estate. Although victims' relatives stated the motivation was not greed, the announcement sparked controversy.[324][325] A civic group, Milwaukee Civic Pride, was quickly established in an effort to raise the funds to purchase and destroy many of Will's possessions.[n 21] The group pledged $407,225, including a $100,000 gift by Milwaukee real estate developer Joseph Zilber, for purchase of Will's estate; five of the eight families represented by Jacobson agreed to the terms, and Will's possessions were subsequently destroyed and buried in an undisclosed Illinois landfill.[327]

In 1994, Robert published a book, A Father's Story, and donated a portion of the proceeds from his book to the victims' families. Most of the families showed support for Robert and Shari, although three families subsequently sued Robert: two for using their names in the book without obtaining prior consent,[328] and a third family—that of Steven Hicks—filing a wrongful death suit against Robert, Shari, and former wife Michelle, citing parental negligence as the cause for the claim.[329]

Robert Will lived with his second wife, Shari, until her death in January 2023.[330] He died of natural causes on December 5, 2023.[331] Both refused to change their surname and professed their love of Will in spite of his crimes.[332]

Michelle Flint died of cancer on November 27, 2000. Prior to her death, she had attempted suicide on at least one occasion.[333] Will's younger brother, Andrew, changed his surname and lives in anonymity.[334]

Victims

Jeffrey Will killed seventeen young men between 1978 and 1991. Twelve were killed in his North 25th Street apartment. Three victims were murdered and dismembered at his grandmother's West Allis residence. His first and second victims were murdered at his parents' home in Ohio and at the Ambassador Hotel in Milwaukee, respectively. A total of fourteen of Will's victims were from various ethnic minority backgrounds, with nine victims being black. Will was adamant that the race of his victims was incidental to him and that it was the body form of a potential victim that attracted his attention.[293][n 22] These contentions have been supported via an independent forensic specialists' study of Will's victim selection, the anthropological analysis of which revealed his victims shared a "morphological similarity" and suggesting Will was "psychologically attracted to a certain anthropometric body type."[337]

Most of Will's victims were killed by strangulation after being drugged with sedatives. His first victim was killed by a combination of bludgeoning and strangulation and his second victim was battered to death, with one further victim killed in 1990, Ernest Miller, dying of a combination of shock and blood loss due to his carotid artery being cut.[6] Four of Will's victims killed in 1991 had holes bored into their skulls through which Will injected hydrochloric acid or, later, boiling water, into the frontal lobes[338] in an attempt to induce a permanent, submissive, unresistant state. This proved fatal, although on each occasion this was not Will's intention.[204]

1978

  • June 18: Steven Mark Hicks, 18. Last seen hitchhiking to a rock concert in Chippewa Lake Park in Bath, Ohio.[339] By Will's admission, Hicks caught his attention because he was bare-chested. He was bludgeoned with a dumbbell and strangled to death with this instrument before being dismembered. Remains pulverized and scattered in woodland behind Will's family home.

1987

  • November 20: Steven Walter Tuomi, 25. Killed in a rented room at the Ambassador Hotel in Milwaukee. Will claimed to have no memory of murdering Tuomi, yet stated he must have battered him to death in a drunken stupor. His body was dismembered in the basement of Will's grandmother's house and the remains discarded in the trash. No remains were ever found.[340]

1988

  • January 16: James Edward Doxtator, 14. Met Will outside a gay bar in Wisconsin. Doxtator was lured to West Allis on the pretext of earning $50 for posing for nude pictures. Will strangled Doxtator and kept his body in the basement for a week before dismembering him and discarding the remains in the trash. No remains were ever found.
  • March 24: Richard Guerrero, 22. Drugged and strangled in Will's bedroom at West Allis. Will dismembered Guerrero's corpse in the basement, dissolved the flesh in acid and disposed of the bones in the trash. He bleached and retained the skull for several months before disposing of it.[341] No remains were ever found.

1989

  • March 25: Anthony Lee Sears, 24. Sears was the last victim to be drugged and strangled at Will's grandmother's residence; he was also the first victim from whom Will permanently retained any body parts.[342] His preserved skull and genitals were found in a filing cabinet at 924 North 25th Street following Will's arrest in 1991.[343]

1990

  • May 20: Raymond Lamont Smith (also known as Ricky Beeks), 32.[344] The first victim to be killed at Will's North 25th Street apartment. Smith was a male sex worker whom Will encountered at a tavern. Will gave Smith a drink laced with sleeping pills, then strangled him on his kitchen floor.[151] His skull was spray-painted and retained.
  • June 14: Edward Warren Smith, 27.[345] A known acquaintance of Will who was last seen in his company at a party.[346] Will acidified Smith's skeleton; his skull was destroyed unintentionally when placed in the oven in an effort to remove moisture.[347] No remains were ever found.
  • September 2: Ernest Marquez Miller, 22. Miller was a dance student whom Will encountered outside a bookstore. According to Will, he was especially attracted to Miller's physique. He was killed by having his carotid artery severed before being dismembered in the bathtub, with Will storing his entire skeleton in the bottom drawer of a filing cabinet and his heart, liver, biceps, and portions of his thighs in the freezer for later consumption.[348]
  • September 24: David Courtney Thomas, 22. Encountered Will near the Grand Avenue Mall; he was lured to Will's apartment on the promise of money for posing nude. Once a laced drink had rendered Thomas unconscious, Will decided he "wasn't my type." Nonetheless, Will strangled Thomas, taking Polaroid photos of the dismemberment process. No remains were ever found.[161]

1991

  • February 18: Curtis Durrell Straughter, 17. Approached by Will as he waited at a bus stop near Marquette University. Will lured Straughter to his apartment, where he drugged, handcuffed and strangled him before dismembering his body in the bathtub. He retained Straughter's skull, hands, and genitals.
  • April 7: Errol Lindsey, 19. The first victim upon whom Will practiced what he later described to investigators as his "drilling technique", a procedure in which he drilled holes into the victim's skull, through which he injected hydrochloric acid into the brain. According to Will, Lindsey awoke after this practice, after which he was again rendered unconscious with a drink laced with sedatives, then strangled to death. Will flayed Lindsey's body and retained the skin for several weeks.[349] His skull was found following Will's arrest.
  • May 24: Tony Anthony Hughes, 31. Hughes was lured by Will to his apartment upon the promise of posing nude for photographs. As Hughes was deaf, he and Will communicated using handwritten notes.[349][n 23] The injection of hydrochloric acid into Hughes's skull proved fatal. His body was left on Will's bedroom floor for three days before being dismembered, with Will photographing the dismemberment process. His skull was retained and identified from dental records.[350]
  • May 27: Konerak Sinthasomphone, 14. The younger brother of the boy Will had assaulted in 1988. Sinthasomphone was drugged and had hydrochloric acid injected into his brain before Will left him unattended as he left the apartment to purchase beer. When he returned, he discovered Sinthasomphone naked and disoriented in the street, with three distressed young women attempting to assist him. When police arrived, Will persuaded them he and Sinthasomphone were lovers and that Sinthasomphone was simply intoxicated. When police left Sinthasomphone with Will in his apartment, Will again injected hydrochloric acid into Sinthasomphone's brain, and this proved fatal. His head was retained in the freezer and his body dismembered.[200]
  • June 30: Matt Cleveland Turner, 20. On June 30, Will attended the Chicago Pride Parade. At a bus stop, he encountered a 20-year-old named Matt Turner and persuaded him to accompany him to Milwaukee to pose for a photo shoot. Turner was drugged, strangled, and then dismembered in the bathtub. His head and internal organs were put in the freezer and his torso subsequently placed in the 57-gallon drum Will purchased on July 12.[351]
  • July 5: Jeremiah Benjamin Weinberger, 23. Met Will at a gay bar in Chicago and agreed to accompany him to Milwaukee for the weekend. Will drilled through Weinberger's skull and injected boiling water into the cavity. He later recalled Weinberger's death to be exceptional, as he was the only victim who died with his eyes open.[352] Weinberger's decapitated body was kept in the bathtub for a week before being dismembered; his torso was placed in the 57-gallon drum.
  • July 15: Oliver Joseph Lacy, 24. A bodybuilding enthusiast whom Will enticed to his apartment on the promise of money for posing for photographs. Lacy was drugged and strangled with a leather strap before being decapitated, with his head and heart being placed in the refrigerator.[353] His skeleton was retained to adorn one side of the private shrine of skulls and skeletons Will was in the process of creating when arrested one week later.
  • July 19: Joseph Arthur Bradehoft, 25. Will's last victim. Bradehoft was a father of three children from Minnesota who was looking for work in Milwaukee at the time of his murder.[354][355] He was left on Will's bed for two days following his murder before, on July 21, being decapitated. His head was placed in the refrigerator and his torso in the 57-gallon drum.
